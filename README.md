# Sprint6Project---Games-Analysis

TripleTen’s Project for the 6th sprint, in which I analyzed sales data of video games from different regions.

In the first graph, we can see that the first 10 years of the video game industry were not very profitable. This can be associated with the fact that video games at that time were marketed mainly toward children, and the scope of the games were too simple and not appealing to the general audience. This is why I believe the early years of the video game industry were relatively weak.

However, from the mid-1990s to the early 2000s, the industry started booming in terms of sales. The PlayStation 1 and PlayStation 2 pushed video game sales significantly, mainly because of their innovations. These included the ability to render 3D graphics much better than previous generations, the use of CDs as a game medium (which were cheap to produce), the PlayStation 2’s ability to read DVDs, and lastly, piracy, which greatly boosted the PlayStation 2’s popularity.

In the second graph, we can see more clearly the sales of games per platform. The first platform to ever reach 100 million game sales was the PlayStation 1. Even though SEGA and Nintendo had dominated the market previously, Sony was the company that truly pushed the industry forward.

In the third graph, we can see more accurately the sales of games per platform in the late 2000s.

In the fourth graph, we see a boxplot showing the average number of sales for each platform and their outliers. We observe that the representation for both the Xbox 360 and the PlayStation 3 is very similar. The main difference is that the PS3 had a few games that did not sell well, while the Xbox 360 had more consistent sales. The Nintendo Wii had the most outliers. Its game library included a lot of shovelware (games that were quickly and cheaply produced). Because it was a console marketed toward a broader audience, many games on this platform did not sell well. Nonetheless, the Nintendo Wii also had a few top-selling games, such as Wii Sports, Mario Kart Wii, and Wii Sports Resort.

The fifth graph, along with the statistical analysis performed in this project, showed that a game’s sales are not strongly affected by user scores, with a correlation of only 8%. Factors such as strong marketing or accessibility have a much greater impact than user ratings.

In the sixth and final graph, there is a comparison of sales for the game Resident Evil 2, which was released on three platforms: the PlayStation 1, the Nintendo 64, and the Nintendo GameCube. There is a significant gap between the two companies. As mentioned earlier in the project, the PS1 had many advantages over the Nintendo 64 and also achieved much higher console sales.
