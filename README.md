TripleTen’s project for the sixth sprint, in which I analyzed video game sales data across different regions.

In the first graph, we can observe that the first ten years of the video game industry were not very profitable. This may be associated with the fact that video games at the time were primarily marketed toward children, and the games themselves were relatively simple and less appealing to the general audience. As a result, the industry experienced slower growth during its early years.

However, from the mid-1990s to the early 2000s, the industry experienced a major boom in sales. The PlayStation 1 and PlayStation 2 significantly boosted video game sales, mainly because of their technological innovations. These included improved 3D graphics rendering, the use of CDs as a cheaper and more efficient game medium, the PlayStation 2’s ability to play DVDs, and piracy, which also contributed greatly to the PS2’s popularity.

In the second graph, we can more clearly observe game sales by platform. The first platform to surpass 100 million game sales was the PlayStation 1. Although SEGA and Nintendo had previously dominated the market, Sony was the company that significantly accelerated the industry's growth.

In the third graph, we can more accurately observe game sales by platform during the late 2000s.

In the fourth graph, we see a boxplot representing the average sales for each platform and their outliers. We can observe that the Xbox 360 and PlayStation 3 have very similar distributions. The main difference is that the PS3 had a few games with very low sales, while Xbox 360 sales were more consistent overall. The Nintendo Wii presented the highest number of outliers. Its game library contained a considerable amount of shovelware — games that were quickly and cheaply produced. Since the console targeted a broader audience, many of its games did not perform well commercially. Nevertheless, the Nintendo Wii also had several top-selling titles, such as Wii Sports, Mario Kart Wii, and Wii Sports Resort.

The fifth graph, along with the statistical analysis conducted in this project, showed that game sales are not strongly affected by user scores, with a correlation of only 8%. Factors such as strong marketing, accessibility, and platform popularity appear to have a much greater impact on sales than user ratings.

The sixth graph compares sales of the game Resident Evil 2, which was released on three platforms: the PlayStation 1, Nintendo 64, and Nintendo GameCube. There is a significant difference in sales performance between the platforms. As previously mentioned, the PS1 had several advantages over the Nintendo 64, which contributed to its substantially higher console and game sales.

The graph comparing the average user scores for Xbox One and PC games, along with the results of the t-test, indicates that there is a statistically significant difference between them. Players tend to enjoy gaming on PC more due to factors such as better graphics, lower game prices, and the possibility of using mods.

Finally, the last graph and the t-test conducted to compare user scores between action and sports games showed a p-value lower than the significance threshold of 0.05. Therefore, we reject the null hypothesis, indicating that there is a statistically significant difference between the scores of these two genres. Although both action and sports games are highly popular and emotionally engaging, action games tend to feature faster-paced gameplay, which may contribute more strongly to players’ excitement and overall gaming experience.
